# Phase 4.1: User Deployment - Dashboard & QuickBooks Data Validation - Context

**Gathered:** 2026-01-12
**Status:** Ready for research and planning

---

<vision>
## How This Should Work

When users from Commercial, Admin, and Finance departments log into the system, they land on a dashboard that's polished, organized, and immediately useful for their job.

The dashboard isn't just a placeholder anymore — it's the control center. Finance sees their invoices and payments. Admin sees system health and configuration. Commercial sees their leads and deals.

All the data they need is there, complete and accurate, synced from QuickBooks with full confidence.

</vision>

<essential>
## What Must Be Nailed

The non-negotiable that makes this phase successful:

- **Dashboard is polished and usable** — Users land on the dashboard and immediately know what to do and where to find their data. No more "this is minimal" feeling.
- **All QuickBooks data is complete** — Every field, every record that matters is being captured from QB. Finance can trust the data. No surprises after deployment.
- **Each department can do their job** — Finance can manage invoices/payments, Admin can monitor the system and QB connection, Commercial can track deals and customer info.

</essential>

<boundaries>
## What's Out of Scope

Explicitly NOT part of this phase:

- **Mobile optimization** — Desktop-first. Mobile comes later.
- **Advanced QuickBooks features** — Just make sure the core sync works and critical fields are captured. Edge cases and advanced QB features defer to future phases.
- **Analytics or reporting** — This phase is about data capture and display, not dashboards with graphs and trending reports.

</boundaries>

<specifics>
## Specific Ideas

**Dashboard improvements needed:**
- Currently too minimal/empty — needs more data, context, and functionality visible
- Should show department-relevant information prominently
- Each department's view should be tailored to their workflow

**QuickBooks data gaps to address:**
- Customer/company data — ensure all customer details are captured
- Invoice data — complete invoice information and status tracking
- Payment data — payment status, history, reconciliation data
- Account/GL data — chart of accounts, financial data visibility

**Department-specific dashboards:**
- **Finance:** Invoices, payments, accounts, GL data, reconciliation capability
- **Admin:** Data sync status, QB connection health, system monitoring, user management, integration logs
- **Commercial:** Leads, deals, pipeline, customer info, revenue tracking

</specifics>

<notes>
## Additional Context

**Priority alignment:**
All three areas (dashboard UX, QB data completeness, department access) are equally critical blockers. Can't deploy without fixing all three.

**Confidence threshold:**
The system is ready for users when the dashboard is polished and usable by all departments — that's the primary measure of success.

**Data validation:**
This is deploy-ready work. Users will trust (or not trust) the system based on whether the data they see is complete and accurate. QB data validation is essential for that trust.

</notes>
